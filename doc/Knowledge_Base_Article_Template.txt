KNOWLEDGE BASE ARTICLE TEMPLATE
===============================

PURPOSE
-------
This template is designed for creating deep technical knowledge base articles
that teach conceptual understanding, engineering intuition, practical insights,
and real-world applications. The writing style should feel like a senior engineer
mentoring a junior engineer rather than traditional documentation.

TITLE
-----
Use a title that clearly communicates both the topic and the practical question it answers.

INTRODUCTION
------------
- Open with a problem, observation, or real-world scenario.
- Avoid starting with textbook definitions.
- Explain why the topic exists and why engineers should care.
- End with: "By the end of this article..."

Writing Goal:
Create curiosity before teaching details.

THE FUNDAMENTAL PROBLEM
-----------------------
Before explaining the solution, explain the problem.

Questions:
- What challenge exists?
- Why is the obvious approach insufficient?
- What limitations drove the creation of this concept?

Writing Goal:
Help readers understand WHY the concept exists.

THE BIG PICTURE
---------------
Provide the highest-level view possible.

Show:
- Overall flow
- System interaction
- Architectural position
- Major components involved

Use:
- Flow diagrams
- Architecture diagrams
- Sequence diagrams

Writing Goal:
Create a mental map before diving deeper.

KEY CONCEPTS AND TERMINOLOGY
----------------------------
Introduce the core vocabulary required for the topic.

Each term should be explained, not merely defined.

Writing Goal:
Give readers the language needed to understand later sections.

HOW IT WORKS
------------
This is the heart of the article.

Walk through the process sequentially:

STEP 1
STEP 2
STEP 3
...

Each step should explain:
- What happens
- Why it happens
- What hardware is involved
- What software is involved

Writing Goal:
Allow readers to mentally simulate the system.

UNDER THE HOOD
--------------
Explain internal behavior.

Focus on:
- CPU behavior
- Memory behavior
- Hardware mechanisms
- Timing implications
- Register-level concepts

Writing Goal:
Move beyond surface-level understanding.

REAL-WORLD APPLICATIONS
-----------------------
Show how the concept appears in actual products.

Examples:
- Automotive systems
- Consumer electronics
- Industrial automation
- Medical devices
- Aerospace systems
- IoT products

Writing Goal:
Connect theory to engineering practice.

COMMON MISTAKES
---------------
Discuss:
- Incorrect assumptions
- Design mistakes
- Configuration errors
- Architectural mistakes

Writing Goal:
Prevent common failures before they occur.

DEBUGGING AND TROUBLESHOOTING
-----------------------------
Structure issues as:

Symptom
Possible Cause
Investigation Method
Resolution

Writing Goal:
Help readers solve real problems.

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS AND BEST PRACTICES
----------------------------------------
Discuss:
- Performance
- Reliability
- Scalability
- Power consumption
- Safety
- Maintainability

Explain WHY each recommendation exists.

Writing Goal:
Teach engineering judgment.

EXPERT NOTES
------------
The most valuable section.

Examples:
- What junior engineers frequently misunderstand
- What causes problems in production systems
- What textbooks often omit
- What experienced engineers pay attention to

Writing Goal:
Make readers feel they are learning from someone who has built real systems.

SUMMARY
-------
Summarize the article in narrative form.

Answer:
- What did we learn?
- Why does it matter?
- What mental model should readers retain?

RELATED TOPICS
--------------
Prerequisites:
Topics that should be understood first.

Next Topics:
Recommended continuation of the learning journey.

GLOBAL WRITING GUIDELINES
-------------------------
1. Avoid textbook definitions at the start.
2. Always explain the problem before the solution.
3. Use storytelling where possible.
4. Teach cause-and-effect relationships.
5. Focus on mental models, not memorization.
6. Use diagrams heavily for architecture and flow topics.
7. Include expert insights not easily found in vendor documentation.
8. Write as a senior engineer mentoring a junior engineer.
9. Prioritize understanding over completeness.
10. Build intuition before implementation details.
